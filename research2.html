Anisotropic Distribution of Subhalos: Coherent Accretion and Inner Orbits

Cosmological simulations provide details for us to understand the Universe. We study galaxy assembly and structure formation using simulations. Due to gravity, dark matter collapse into haloes. Galaxies are assembled and reside in dark matter haloes, while small haloes merge to form more massive haloes. Anisotropic distribution of subhaloes have been observed in different simulation, and it may have implications for gravitational lensing, galaxy quenching, predicting galaxy & halo shape, etc. A commonly proprosed cause to the anisotropic distribution is the anisotropic accretion along the cosmic filaments. In this study, we use simulation data from IllustrisTNG to answer the questions on:

  • Coherence of accretion along the filament
  • Relative position of Subhaloes to the major axis
  • Subhalo orbits after accretion and their physical implication
